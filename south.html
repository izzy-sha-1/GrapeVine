South London


Sometimes I find being in London gets a bit much with the air pollution and constant traffic. Anywhere I can escape to?!

God I know! It gets to me too. I would recommend Kew Gardens for a little escape. I went at Christmas time when they had a really cool light display but I would say it is well worth a visit any time of year! There’s so much going on yet it still feels like a calm retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life. If you are a big fan of flowers you can go on the website (http://www.kew.org): there’s a cool section dedicated ‘what to see this week’.

I would love to go to a Shakespeare production but don’t want to have to fork out a tonne of money for it. The cheaper seats are always so far way! I want to be able to get a good look at the action!

Heya. Have you considered screening of plays? I am a convert. You can watch a great performance on much comfier cinema seats and not be judged for munching on popcorn either! I recently went to watch a screening of ‘The Twelfth Night’ from the National Theatre but I know there is a screening coming up in Clapham on 6th April. Adult tickets are about £20 and kids half the price at £10.

Ok so this place is slightly more central than South but it’s well worth the trip. Last week I went to Kouzu for a fancy Japanese style afternoon tea. It was so tasty and such a refreshing spin on the generic English afternoon tea. Even better, I found a deal on Groupon and got the whole thing for half the price.
